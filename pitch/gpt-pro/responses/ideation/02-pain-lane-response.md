Working title: ProofDeck Room

Seven-word rumor: Founder updates once; investors reopen living proof.

Specific reachable user: Pre-seed or seed founder raising from angels, accelerators, or small funds.

Demand sentence: We help early founders who repeatedly want investor-ready credibility, faster follow-up, and less narrative drift by letting them drop one traction update into a durable agent-run deal room, possible now because agent memory can persist across sessions and Walrus can make source artifacts portable and inspectable, and this can become a company because fundraising rooms wedge into diligence workspaces, investor Q&A memory, accelerator cohorts, and governed startup data rooms.

Desire / power pull: Look sharper than the company’s actual operational chaos; never lose the strongest proof, answer, or investor objection again.

Tuesday pull: After every customer win, metric change, pilot update, or investor call, the founder updates the room once and the agent repairs the pitch, Q&A, and evidence trail.

Public or team-visible artifact: Shareable investor room with a redacted proof timeline, claim cards, source blobs, Q&A memory, and “open as investor” replay.

Beachhead demo case: Founder changes MRR, churn, or customer segment; the agent rewrites the investor claim, updates the risk answer, stores the evidence packet on Walrus, and reopens it from an investor view.

Judge first action <=30s: Edit “MRR jumped from $8k to $14k but churn rose to 9%” and press Update Room.

First 5-second replayable clip: Traction card mutates → investor objection appears → source receipt writes to Walrus → “open as investor” recalls the exact updated claim.

What changes when judge changes input: Ask size, investor memo, risk flags, follow-up email, claim confidence, room version, and recalled Q&A all change.

Walrus or MemWal critical path: Founder facts, source files, investor objections, claim versions, and delegate access live in a Walrus/MemWal memory space. Removing Walrus collapses the product into a local pitch generator with no durable investor room, no cross-session recall, no verifiable evidence object, and no delegated reopen.

Agent-native loop: Goal is to keep the deal room investor-ready; agent observes changed metrics and investor questions, plans which claims need repair, calls tools, writes durable room state, verifies sources, then asks founder approval before publishing.

Agent tools >=3: Metrics calculator; source/evidence importer; investor persona/Q&A generator; follow-up email composer; Walrus write/read/delegate; room replay inspector.

Durable state objects >=3: Founder fact ledger; claim-source blob set; investor objection memory; room version history; delegate link; replay trace.

Surprise / replan path: Judge changes “enterprise pilots” to “unpaid LOIs”; agent downgrades claim strength, rewrites the investor answer, marks missing proof, and asks for human approval before sharing.

What judges can inspect: Walrus blob/object ID, source attachment, room version diff, investor-view recall, delegated access record, and agent trace.

Product thesis: Fundraising is a recurring memory and evidence problem, not a one-off deck-generation problem.

Three adjacent cases: Accelerator demo day rooms; grant application rooms; M&A or acquihire diligence rooms.

Expansion ladder: Beachhead founder update room → month-1 investor Q&A memory workspace → month-6 governed data-room network for accelerators, angels, and startup operators.

Industry buyer behind it: Accelerators, founder platforms, small funds, fractional CFOs, and startup law firms.

Six-month moat or compounding data: Reusable investor objection graph, claim-to-source mappings, founder narrative versions, and sector-specific diligence memories.

Nearest boring clone: AI pitch deck generator or investor CRM dashboard.

Mutation that keeps it judge-magnetic: The first beat is not “make a deck”; it is “change one private company fact and watch the investor room remember, repair, and reopen.”

Why it might still lose: The judge could read it as a polished startup assistant unless the Walrus reopen/delegate path is unmistakable in the first minute.

Lane Self-Red-Team

Strongest lane candidate: I2-7 BidForge Memory as the strongest lane candidate, not a final tournament pick. It has direct money upside, an instantly understandable judge action, recurring Tuesday use, a real buyer, durable artifact value, and a Walrus object that cannot be reduced to optional storage.

Candidate to kill immediately: I2-6 PracticeProof Coach unless the tournament needs a consumer/status lane. It is emotionally legible, but the buyer and budget are softer, and it risks collapsing into an AI coach with saved clips.

Repeated trap this lane must avoid: Turning Walrus into “saved output storage” after a generic agent does the work. The durable object has to change later behavior, support reopen/recall/share/delegate/inspect, and survive a judge-controlled mutation; otherwise the project becomes the exact hidden-memory/dashboard clone the research warned against.

Evidence from R0 that most constrained your choices: The R0 pack says the most judge-legible hooks are remembered facts, reopened work, second-agent/shared memory, resumed workflows, and proof tied to durable objects; it also says repeated buyer signals cluster around enterprise/team controls, compliance-sensitive workflows, revenue-critical logs, coding productivity, and data ownership.
